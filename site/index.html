Why this exists

Your AI agent is doing more than you think

AI coding agents don't just respond to your prompts. Behind every session, background processes run silently: advisor models that pre-screen your requests, "dreaming" processes that reorganize context, classifiers that categorize your work, and anti-distillation checks that protect model weights.

These background agents consume tokens, write persistent memory files, and phone home to remote servers—all without surfacing in your session UI. Your bill says one thing; reality is another.

agentlens exists because developers deserve to see what's running on their machines. No judgment, no drama—just visibility.

  • 100% local—nothing leaves your machine
  • Read-only—never modifies agent data
  • Open source under MIT license
  • Currently supports Claude Code, more agents coming

What are background agents?

When you start a coding session, the AI agent may spawn additional model calls you never asked for:

advisor — A secondary model that evaluates your prompt before the primary model sees it.

dreaming — An offline process that consolidates session context into long-term memory.

classifier — Lightweight models that tag and route your requests internally.

anti-distillation — Checks designed to detect if outputs are being used to train competing models.

Each of these consumes tokens that may appear on your usage bill without clear attribution.
